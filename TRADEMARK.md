# ATLAS Trademark & Naming Policy (Draft)

This document explains how names and branding should be used to prevent user
confusion, especially when forks modify scoring behavior.

## ATLAS marks

The following are treated as ATLAS marks:
- “ATLAS”
- “ATLAS score”
- the ATLAS logo and associated branding

## What you may do

- Use and fork the code under the project license.
- Describe compatibility or origin factually (e.g., “Forked from the ATLAS project”).

## What you may NOT do without permission

- Present a modified fork as the official ATLAS project.
- Use ATLAS marks in product naming/branding in a way that implies endorsement.
- If you materially change the scoring process, you may not present outputs as “ATLAS scores” without validation from the ATLAS project that it is acceptable. It should also have a prominent qualification and a distinct fork name.

## Recommended fork naming

If you modify scoring rules or evaluation logic, use naming like:
- “ATLAS-compatible explorer (Fork: <YourName>)”
- “Derived from ATLAS (Modified Scoring)”

Avoid naming like:
- “ATLAS Score”
- “Official ATLAS”
