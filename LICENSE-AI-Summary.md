## 💰 The Commercial Use Rules (The Part Most Businesses Need to Read)

### The $1M Free Tier

If you're using ATLAS commercially — meaning you're making money from it, or it saves your business significant costs — **you can use it for free as long as your "Attributable Value" stays under $1,000,000 USD in any 12-month period.**

**"Attributable Value" means:**
- Revenue your business earned because of this software, **plus**
- Cost savings the software gave you (e.g., you didn't have to hire staff or buy another tool).

> **Important:** If the software is a key part of a product or service, you must count at least 10% of that product's total revenue/savings as Attributable Value — you can't claim it barely contributed.

### What Happens When You Hit $1M?

Before you cross the $1M threshold (or within 30 days of crossing it), you must either:
1. **Stop using it commercially**, or
2. **Get a paid commercial license** from the ATLAS Foundation.

Continuing to use it past $1M without a license is a breach of the agreement.

### Don't Try to Game the System

You can't split your business into subsidiaries or shell companies to get each one under the $1M cap. The license explicitly says the ATLAS Foundation can look through these structures and treat the whole enterprise as one entity.

### Affiliates Count Too

If your parent company, subsidiaries, or sister companies are also using the software, their Attributable Value gets **added to yours** for the purposes of the $1M cap — unless they each have their own commercial license.

## 🌍 The Public-Benefit / ACeS Exception (Nonprofits & Mission-Aligned Orgs)

If your organization qualifies, the $1M commercial cap **does not apply to you at all.**

**To qualify, you must meet ALL of these:**

1. **You're a real nonprofit.** Incorporated as a nonprofit, charity, foundation, or cooperative — recognized (or in the process of being recognized) as tax-exempt by your government. No distributing profits to owners.

2. **No private profit.** You don't pay dividends or distribute profits to individuals or for-profit entities. Reasonable salaries for actual work are fine.

3. **Mission alignment.** Your founding documents state that your primary purpose is expanding access to resources, technology, or knowledge to reduce scarcity and increase human flourishing — what the license calls an **"Abundance Centered Society" (ACeS)** or **"Post-Scarcity Society"** mission. And your actual work must match that mission.

**How to claim the exception:**
- Send a self-certification letter to the ATLAS Foundation within 30 days of claiming this exception (see contact details at the bottom).
- *Alternatively*, get a certificate from the **Abundant Mars Foundation** or the **Non-Reductionist Philosophy organisation**.
- Update them promptly if your situation changes.

The ATLAS Foundation can review your certification and give you 60 days to fix any issues before the commercial cap kicks in.

## 📋 Rules Everyone Must Follow (Including Nonprofits)

### Attribution — You Must Credit ATLAS

If you share or publish the software (or a modified version), you must:

1. **Include the original license** — keep the LICENSE.md file (or a clear link to it) in what you distribute.
2. **Say you've modified it** — if you changed anything, include a visible notice in your README or documentation saying it's a modified fork of ATLAS and is not the official release.
3. **Describe your changes** — if you changed how the scoring or evaluation works, write a clear explanation of what you changed so a technical person could understand the difference from the original.
4. **Don't remove copyright notices** — leave all the original ATLAS copyright and trademark notices in place.

### Trademark — Don't Pretend to Be Official ATLAS

- You **cannot** use the ATLAS name, logo, or "ATLAS score" branding in a way that implies your version is the official ATLAS product.
- You **cannot** call your modified scoring method an "ATLAS score" without a clear disclaimer that it was produced by a modified process — and you need written permission to use the "ATLAS" name in your product name.
- You **can** say things like: *"This product incorporates code derived from the ATLAS project (atlasscore.org)"* — that's fine.

## 🚫 When Your License Gets Cancelled

Your right to use ATLAS is automatically cancelled if you materially break the rules. The main ways that can happen:

- Exceeding the $1M threshold without a commercial license
- Misusing the ATLAS trademarks
- Failing to include required attribution or modification notices

**You get 30 days to fix it** if the problem is fixable. If you fix it in time, it's treated as if the breach didn't happen. After cancellation, the ATLAS Foundation can choose to reinstate you on their terms.
